## ✅ Investor Meeting Checklist

### Before the Meeting
- [ ] Research the investor (portfolio, focus areas, check size)
- [ ] Customize deck for their interests
- [ ] Prepare specific questions for them
- [ ] Test technology (Zoom, screen sharing)
- [ ] Have backup materials ready to share

### During the Meeting
- [ ] Start with 30-second pitch
- [ ] Ask about their background/interests
- [ ] Walk through deck (10-12 min max)
- [ ] Leave time for questions (most important part)
- [ ] Be honest about risks/challenges
- [ ] Ask about next steps/timeline

### After the Meeting
- [ ] Send thank you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Include requested materials
- [ ] Follow up in 1 week if no response
- [ ] Track in CRM/spreadsheet

## ✅ Ideal Investor Profile

### Who You Want
- **Check Size:** $25k-$100k (angel) or $250k (seed fund)
- **Focus:** SaaS, AI, SMB tech, automation
- **Stage:** Pre-seed/seed, pre-revenue OK
- **Value-Add:** Connections to SMBs, AI expertise, fundraising help
- **Geography:** Open to remote (you're remote-first)

### Where to Find Them
1. **AngelList** – Create profile, list round
2. **LinkedIn** – Search "AI investor", "SaaS investor"
3. **Twitter** – Follow #angelinvesting, #SaaS
4. **Local Events** – Startup meetups (virtual/IRL)
5. **Warm Intros** – Ask network for introductions
6. **Cold Outreach** – Personalized emails to investors

### Red Flags
- Investor wants >20% for seed round
- No experience in your space
- Slow decision-making (>2 months)
- Unreasonable terms (liquidation preferences >1x)
- No value-add beyond money

## ✅ Next Steps After Funding

### First 30 Days
1. **Legal:** Set up SAFE/note, bank account, cap table
2. **Team:** Hire first automation specialist
3. **Product:** Begin SaaS MVP development
4. **Sales:** Ramp up outreach, aim for first DFY client
5. **Reporting:** Set up monthly investor updates

### Investor Updates (Monthly)
- **Progress vs. milestones**
- **Key metrics** (MRR, CAC, LTV, churn)
- **Challenges & solutions**
- **Cash runway**
- **Ask for help** (intros, advice, etc.)

### Series A Preparation (Month 6+)
- **Target:** $20k MRR
- **Metrics:** Strong unit economics, growth rate
- **Materials:** Updated deck, financials, case studies
- **Network:** Start warm introductions to Series A funds

## ✅ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-optimistic projections** – Be realistic
2. **Hiding risks** – Investors appreciate honesty
3. **Too much jargon** – Speak plainly
4. **Not knowing your numbers** – Memorize key metrics
5. **Asking for wrong amount** – $250k is reasonable for seed
6. **No clear use of funds** – Be specific about where money goes
7. **Ignoring investor fit** – Target those who understand your space
8. **Poor follow-up** – Timeliness matters
